About

I'm a game designer and developer with 5 years of hands-on experience building games across simulation, management, and detective genres. I graduated with a First-Class Honours degree in Computer Games Technology from the University of Portsmouth.

My work spans the full production pipeline, from writing game design documents and designing gameplay loops to implementing and iterating on systems in Unity and Unreal Engine 5. I approach design with a research-first mindset, drawing on theories of game feel, player agency, and immersion to make informed decisions.

I have worked in teams and solo across industry partnerships with Frontier Developments and personal projects, with my work showcased at EGX, Barclays Game Frenzy London, and GDLX 2025.

Detective / Deduction Game Final Year Project UE5

Murder in the Whitewoods

A solo-designed and developed first-person deduction detective game, built to explore how game feel and player agency can create immersion. The project included a full Game Design Document and a completed prototype built in Unreal Engine 5.

View GDD Prototype on request
Design
  • Made a complete GDD covering the gameplay loop, level layout, and mechanics
  • Designed a deduction-style investigative loop: exploration, clue collection, a notebook for evidence and statements, and an open suspect accusation with an outcome reveal
  • Designed player agency through consequence-driven freedom, giving players control over when to accuse with missed content surfaced post-game
  • Level designed a multi-room manor and grounds with looping paths, contextually placed clues, and audio cues to guide exploration
  • Applied proactive scope management, cutting features mid-development to protect core quality
Production
  • Built a full working prototype in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprints, covering player movement, clue interaction, audio cues, notebook UI, and the suspect accusation system
  • Ran structured playtesting sessions and used findings to evaluate and iterate on the design
  • Managed the project using a hybrid waterfall and agile methodology, with clear milestones and rapid prototype-and-test cycles for individual mechanics
  • Produced supporting academic research applying frameworks on game feel, flow theory, and player agency to design decisions throughout the project
